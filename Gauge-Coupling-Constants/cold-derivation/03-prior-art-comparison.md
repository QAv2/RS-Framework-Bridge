### 3.2 e×π as dual to Euler's identity

**Session 8 framing**:

> Understanding e×π
> We established that **e×π is dual to Euler's identity**:
> - **e^(iπ) = -1** encodes rotation as exponential (dynamics)
> - **e × π = 8.54** encodes rotation as action×phase (coupling strength)
>

**Cold pass coverage**: cold pass §3.3 invoked BCH-style non-commutative product reasoning to motivate the e·π factor in αs = 137/(136·e·π). The cold pass argued that non-commuting quaternion axes generate exponential factors via Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff.

**Prior pass tightening**: the e^(iπ) ↔ e·π duality is *cleaner* than the BCH argument. e^(iπ) = -1 is a literal RS reading (1D rotation by π brings +1 to -1, i.e., material datum to counterspace datum per Peret EE §5). The "magnitude version" e·π is the corresponding action × phase product.

The "each of 3 dimensions contributes e^(1/3) × π/3" reading (Session 8) is interesting. (e^(1/3))³ × (π/3)³ = e × π³/27 — not e·π directly. So this specific reading needs more checking. But the e^(iπ) duality framing is robust.

The cold pass should absorb the dual-Euler reading. The BCH argument can stay as a complementary "why this kind of factor" justification, but the duality is the clearer structural identification.

### 3.3 Bare-coupling-limit hypothesis (GUT scale)

**Session 8 framing**:

> - sin²θW → 1/4 (no "+1")

This is a *falsifiable hypothesis* about the bare-coupling limit. Cold pass entirely missed this direction.

This is a real prediction. It's testable by computing the master-formula ratio at different energy scales and seeing whether the offset approaches zero as scale → ∞.

The cold pass should absorb this as a falsifiable signature, similar to the Bhandari 2nπ phase memory in HP#4 §7 S5.

### 3.4 G2 (14-dim exceptional Lie group) connection

**Session 8 framing**:

> **H5**: Connection to quaternion structure (RS2)
> - 14 = dimension of the G2 exceptional Lie group
> - √14 might relate to octonion/quaternion geometry in RS2

This is a deeper structural pointer. G2 is the automorphism group of the octonions ('s algebra of imaginary unit-vectors). dim(G2) = 14 = 1² + 2² + 3².

In RS2, the atomic zone is 4D quaternion (LB2), and Cayley-Dickson doubling extends to octonions ℍ → 𝕆 (Nehru §8 implicit). If RS2's eventual deep structure involves octonions, then G2 (the octonion automorphism group) becomes RS2-natural. The √14 = √(dim G2) = ||(1,2,3)||₂ reading then has TWO interpretations:

(a) Euclidean norm of (1, 2, 3) dimension vector — surface reading.
(b) Square root of dim G2 (octonion automorphism group) — deeper RS2 reading.

Both give the same number. If RS2 is fundamentally octonionic, (b) is the load-bearing reading. The cold pass missed both; should absorb at least the surface reading and forward-flag the G2 reading.
