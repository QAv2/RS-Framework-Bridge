## HP#1 epistemic class

HP#1 is in the **qualitative class** — not a closed-form numerical prediction (like HP#2 Yang-Mills mass gap or HP#4 FSC at 2.2 ppm) and not a structural identification of an operator (like HP#7 Riemann's Berry-Keating). Instead, HP#1 is a **reframing argument**: in the conventional Navier-Stokes Millennium Problem, the question is whether smooth solutions exist for all time. RS2 reframes this — under discrete-unit space and time, the question "does vorticity blow up to infinity?" may be ill-posed because infinite gradients are forbidden by the unit-boundary discretization itself.

The cold pass for HP#1 is testing whether RS2 produces a structurally-grounded reading of why NS regularity should hold (or where the conventional question is ill-posed in RS2 terms), not a Millennium-grade construction proof. This is consistent with Joe's framing 2026-04-29 evening: "the qualitative use for RS2 as a reframing device deserves a place in the series."
