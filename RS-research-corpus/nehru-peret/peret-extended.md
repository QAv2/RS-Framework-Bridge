## "Subatomic Mass, Recalculated" (1995) — distilled ⭐⭐⭐

### Setup

Peret takes Larson's mass-component framework from *Nothing But Motion* and recomputes against 1994 *Physical Review D, Particles and Fields* values (more accurate than what Larson had in 1979).

> conversion factor = 0.00054857990 u / 0.00054874099 n = **0.99970644 u/n**

This is the natural-unit-to-12C-unified-atomic-mass-unit conversion, calibrated against the charged electron mass (the most accurate measured particle mass).

Peret's claim: the **observed proton mass is a 50/50 mix of charged and uncharged protons**. The split would be 50.7267% charged, 49.2733% uncharged to reproduce observed precisely. Conventional physics has no charged/uncharged proton distinction, so the measured proton mass is a statistical aggregate.

### Neutrino reinterpretation

Peret diverges from Larson on neutrino mass:
- **Muon neutrino mass = e/2 = 0.27 MeV** (not Larson's full e). Reasoning: the muon-neutrino M ½-½-0 has 2D-distributed potential mass, and `1² = 1` so the mass is `e` not `p+m`, distributed over 2 dim.
- **Electron neutrino has ZERO rest mass**, but the observed 5.1 eV is due to charge-charge interaction `(C−c)² × (nu→u) = 5.21 eV`. **The electron neutrino is an "e-e" combination — the additional space rotation neutralizes the basic time rotation.**

This is significant: in RS2 the electron neutrino's "mass" is purely a measurement artifact of the charged-particle interaction in the detector.

## "The Tao of Larson" (Peret 2016) — distilled

The shortest, cleanest summary of the RS topology:

> "The yin-yang symbol has four distinct regions separated by three boundaries within its circle of completeness (7 parts). White customarily represents yang and equates to space, whereas yin is black, the reciprocal aspect of time."
> "Larson does exactly the same thing, four regions defining where motion takes place, contained by three boundaries, to create his 'completeness' of the universe of motion."

The four regions, mapped:
1. **Yang macrocosm** (large white) = material sector outside region — translational, 1D linear, unbounded, defined by `v¹`
2. **Yin macrocosm** (large black) = cosmic sector outside region — translational analog
3. **Yin microcosm** (small black inside white) = material time region — rotational, bounded, "structure of matter," defined by `v²`
4. **Yang microcosm** (small white inside black) = cosmic space region — antimatter / c-matter

Three boundaries:
- **Unit speed boundary** (the wavy line) = speed of light, separates material from cosmic sector
- **Unit space boundary** (small circle in white) = `1 fm`-ish scale where time region begins
- **Unit time boundary** (small circle in black) = cosmic analog

> "Translational (linear) motion is unbounded ... defined by the first power of speed, velocity (v¹)."
> "Rotational motion is bounded ... defined by the second power of speed (v²), orbital speed."

This is Peret's shorthand for the **second-power dependence** that Nehru also uses: time-region quantities are second powers of S-frame quantities for 1D rotation, fourth powers for 2D rotation.
