# Quaternion Organon — Important Correction

## TL;DR

**"Quaternion Organon" is NOT a Peret book.** It's a 2001 novel by **George Hamner**, full title *Quaternion Organon: A Journey of One Eternal Round*, ISBN `1588380408`, NewSouth Books.

If Joe's prior memory note that the Nehru spin-paper photo source was the "Peret 'Quaternion Organon' book/site" — that is a misattribution. The Nehru spin paper (1997) is canonically published in *Reciprocity* XXVI № 3 p. 15 and the PDF is on `reciprocalsystem.org`. There is no Peret book called Quaternion Organon.

## What the book actually is

Per Publishers Weekly summary and the NewSouth Books page:

> Hamner named his book as the next in line and the natural successor to Aristotle's *Organon*, Bacon's *Novum Organum*, and Ouspensky's *Tertium Organum*.

The plot:
> Two men buy motorcycles and hit the road after being fired during downsizing, looking for meaning in their middle age as they join an odd self-help group headed by a beautiful woman to begin their journey to enlightenment.

It is described as "an odd mixture of cosmology, religion and science fiction."

Hamner's purpose: "to help bring Larson's Reciprocal System to a popular audience." A substantial portion of the book is devoted to Larson's ideas, with footnotes addressing the theory.

The book is **fiction**. Per `reciprocalsystem.org/books`: "Quaternion Organon: A Journey of One Eternal Round by George F. Hamner (2001) – described as 'a fictional novel' with footnotes addressing Larson's theory."

## Hamner's actual published RS papers

George Hamner has TWO papers on the canonical site (separate from the novel):
1. **"Crossing the Quantum Boundary: A Phenomenon of the Astral Plane?"**
2. **"Reflections of a New Member"**

Both are short member-contribution pieces. Neither is technical mass-derivation work.
