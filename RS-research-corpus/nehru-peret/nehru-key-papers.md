## 2. "The Inter-Regional Ratio"
*Reciprocity* XIV № 2 p. 5, 1985. ⭐⭐⭐ **The mass-conversion factor calculation.**

The **inter-regional ratio R** is the multiplicative factor between time-region quantities (inside one natural unit of space) and outside-region (S-frame) measurements. It is what makes RS calibration to MeV work.

For a unit of one-dimensional rotation in three-dimensional space/time, Nehru counts **degrees of freedom**:

> Eq.(2): `f = pⁿ` for vector-dim space (here p=2 directions, n=3 dims → 8 d.o.f. per 1D rotation)
> Eq.(8): `f = p^n / d` for d-dimensional motion in n-dim space

For the atomic structure (two 2D-rotations + one 1D-electric rotation):
> Eq.(9): `(2³/2) × (2³/2) × (2³/1) = 4 × 4 × 8 = **128**`

This **128** is the count of rotational orientational possibilities for an atom in the three-dimensional time of the inside region.

**Vibrational degrees of freedom** add a small correction. For 2D rotation founded on 1D vibration, total vibrational d.o.f. = 3² = 9. For atomic rotation (two 2D rotational systems) the additional d.o.f. is 2/9. For sub-atomic (one 2D rotational system) it is 1/9.

### Final values

> Eq.(14): **Atomic R = 128 + (128 × 2/9) = 156.4444**
> Eq.(15): **Sub-atomic R = 128 + (128 × 1/9) = 142.2222**

Both will appear repeatedly:
- 156.44 is THE inter-regional conversion factor for atomic-zone observations
- 142.22 for sub-atomic-particle-zone

Nehru cautions terminology: "I feel that the word 'orientation' that we have been using in this context does not seem to be too fitting … 'possibility' seems more appropriate. … 'eigenstate.' But 'degrees of freedom' seems very much suitable, providing we refrain from smuggling in some of its spatial connotations."
