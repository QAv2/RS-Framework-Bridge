## Postulates

The two-postulate form (NBM era) is given on the welcome page of reciprocalsystem.org:

> **First Postulate**: "The physical universe is composed of one component, motion, existing in three dimensions, in discrete units and with two, reciprocal aspects, space and time."
>
> **Second Postulate**: "The physical universe conforms to the relations of ordinary, commutative mathematics, its primary magnitudes are absolute and its geometry is Euclidean."

(Cf. SPU 1959 had a slightly different first postulate using "space-time" as a single substance term: "The physical universe is composed entirely of one component, space-time, existing in three dimensions, in discrete units, and in two reciprocal forms, space and time." The 1979 substitution of "motion" for "space-time" is a meaningful late shift — the 1979 form puts motion as ontologically primary, space and time as derived aspects.)

## Ch 09 — Rotational Combinations

URL: `/09-rotational-combinations`

Photons combined with rotation become the rotational base of all material structure.

> "While it has no effective net total magnitude, its rotational component does have a direction."

Critical geometric facts:

> "a photon cannot rotate around its vibration axis, but can rotate around either or both of the two axes perpendicular to the line of vibration and to each other."

> "One rotation generates a two-dimensional figure: a disk. Rotation of the disk around the second available axis then generates a three-dimensional figure: a sphere."

This is RS's geometric reason for two-dimensional basic rotation = "magnetic" and one-dimensional reverse rotation = "electric".

Combination rule (load-bearing):

> "The combined magnitude of two one-dimensional rotations of n displacement units each is 2n. The magnitude of a two-dimensional rotation in which the displacement is n in each dimension is n²."

The `n²` rule for 2D rotation is the seed of the periodic-table 4n²/2n² formula in Ch 10.

## Ch 10 — Atoms

URL: `/10-atoms`

Atoms are double-rotating systems:

> "two photons can rotate around the same central point without interference if the rotational speeds are the same, thus forming a double unit."

A-B-C displacement notation defined:

- `a, b` = displacements in two dimensions of basic two-dimensional rotation (magnetic)
- `c` = speed displacement of one-dimensional reverse rotation (electric)

Verbatim mapping rule:

> "one-dimensional rotation is connected with electrical phenomena, and the two-dimensional rotation is similarly connected with magnetic phenomena."

The **2n² formula** verbatim:

> "A magnetic displacement n is equivalent to 2n² electric displacement units."

Expansion: with n=1,2,3,4 → equivalents 2, 8, 18, 32. The series 2, 8, 18, 32 multiplied by 2 (because two magnetic displacements per atom) gives **4, 16, 36, 64**, i.e., the 4n² formula's full periodic-table block sizes. Joe's `4n²` envelope test in Riemann §7.3 originates here. Inert gas atomic numbers fall at: **2, 10, 18, 36, 54, 86, 118**.

> "the horizontal periods reflect the magnetic rotational displacement, while the vertical groups represent the electric rotational displacement."

Element examples:
- Hydrogen: `2-1-(1)` — single negative electric displacement unit subtracted from helium's base
- Helium: `2-1-0` — minimum atomic structure
- Carbon: `2-1-4` or `2-2-(4)` — dual structural possibility at midpoint
- Argon: `3-2-0`

> "the series of observed chemical elements emerges from the general properties of mathematics and geometry, without making any supplementary assumptions or introducing any numerical values specifically applicable to matter."

This is Larson's strongest claim of "deduction without empirical input". Whether YM mass gap can be derived in the same style is the open question.

## Ch 11 — Sub-Atomic Particles

URL: `/11-sub-atomic-particles`

Sub-atomic particles are rotational structures with insufficient displacement to form a complete atom. The a-b-c notation extends to them with `M` or `C` prefix indicating material or cosmic rotational base.

Particle structures:
- **Electron**: `M 0-0-(1)` — one unit of negative electric displacement on material base. "in effect, a rotating unit of space."
- **Positron**: `M 0-0-1` — positive electric displacement; "essentially a rotating unit of time."
- **Massless neutron**: `M ½-½-0` — two-dimensional magnetic rotation structure.
- **Neutrino**: `M ½-½-(1)` — adding negative electric to massless neutron; "net total displacement of zero."
- **Proton**: `M 1-1-(1)` — combination yielding "particle with an effective mass of one unit."
- **Compound neutron**: contains proton-type and neutrino-type rotations; "one unit of mass."

**Massless threshold** verbatim:

> "None of the particles thus far considered meets this requirement. The electron and the positron have effective rotation in one dimension; the massless neutron in two. The neutrino has no net displacement at all."

Mass requires "net positive three-dimensional rotational displacement". This is the canonical mass-gap analog in RS: **mass exists iff three-dimensional rotation exists**. Below the three-dim threshold, particles are massless.

The Δt ∈ {0,1,2} scheme that Joe references in his memory is RS2-extension; canonical NBM Ch 11 has it as a 3D rotational existence test, not a discrete temporal-dimension count.
