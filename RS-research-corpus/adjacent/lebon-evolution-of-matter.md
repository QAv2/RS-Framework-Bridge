## Author and context

**Gustave Le Bon** (1841–1931): French polymath best known to historians for *The Crowd: A Study of the Popular Mind* (1895), but in scientific circles also for his pre-relativity work on radioactivity, atomic dissociation, and what he called "intra-atomic energy." *Evolution of Matter* (1905 in French; 1907 English translation by F. Legge) and the sequel *Evolution of Forces* (1907 French; 1908 English) were Le Bon's attempt to build a unified picture of matter, energy, and ether on the cusp of the relativistic revolution that would obsolete much of his terminology.

## Table of contents — *Evolution of Matter* (1907 English ed.)

The book is in **six "Books"**:

- **Book I** — *The New Ideas on Matter* (the case that classical mechanics' invariable matter is wrong)
- **Book II** — *Intra-Atomic Energy and Forces Derived Therefrom*
- **Book III** — *The World of the Imponderable* (radiations, "effluves," cathode rays)
- **Book IV** — *The Dematerialization of Matter* (six chapters detailing the dissociation process)
- **Book V** — *The Intermediate World Between Matter and Ether*
- **Book VI** — *The World of Ponderability: Birth, Evolution, and End of Matter*

## Le Bon's definition of mass

**The relevant passage** (Book I, Ch. I, p. 17 of the 1907 Walter Scott ed.):

> "The invariability of the mass of a given quantity of substance — that is to say, its coefficient of inertia measured by its weight, remained the sole irreducible characteristic of matter."

This is Le Bon's framing of the *classical* view he is about to attack: at the time he wrote, the most-defended classical doctrine was that mass is conserved and is the unchanging signature of matter. Le Bon's whole argument is that radioactive dissociation has shown this view false:

> "matter which dissociates dematerializes itself by passing through successive phases which gradually deprive it of its material qualities"
> (*Evolution of Matter*, p. 7)

> "They lose every quality of matter, including the most fundamental of them all — weight."
> (Book IV, on dematerialization)

## Le Bon's positive equation for mass

In *Evolution of Forces* Le Bon gives the **only mass equation** in either book:

> **M = P / g**
>
> "The mass which serves to characterize matter is only the measure of its inertia."

That is: mass equals weight (P, *poids*) divided by gravitational acceleration (g). This is just Newton's second law rearranged — `F = mg ⇒ m = F/g`. Le Bon emphasizes that this ratio is *constant globally*, so mass survives variation of gravity by location.

**This is not the equation `p/v = M`.** Le Bon uses P for *weight*, not p for *momentum*. His treatment is dimensionally Newtonian: mass = inertia, period.

## On `p/v = M` as Peret's re-derivation

Peret's hand-written EE-dictionary entry (Joe's photograph IMG_5295..5298) records the relation:

> p/v = M

where p is momentum and v is velocity. **Crucially, this is Peret's interpretation in the spirit of Le Bon, not a Le Bon quote.** What Le Bon actually says — directly relevant to Peret's claim — is the much more radical position:

> "all mass is mass of the ether, all momentum is momentum of the ether, and all kinetic energy is kinetic energy of the ether"
> (paraphrase from *Evolution of Forces*, as quoted in the Borderland Sciences review)

Translation into Peret/Larson language: there is no "matter" carrying momentum independently. There is just the ether (= the field of motion in space-time, in RS), and mass is a *derived* quantity from the more primary momentum-divided-by-velocity relation. Peret's `p/v = M` is the dimensional restatement that *if* momentum p (motion through the ether) is the primary quantity, then mass is the conjugate of velocity to that momentum — a derived, not given, quantity.

This is consistent with Larson's RS axiom: "the universe is composed of one component, motion."

## Le Bon's eight foundational principles (*Evolution of Forces*, Part I)

Recovered from the rexresearch.com mirror:

1. **Matter vanishes** through continuous atomic dissociation
2. **Dematerialization** produces substances bridging the ponderable and imponderable realms
3. **Matter is an enormous intra-atomic energy reservoir**
4. **Intra-atomic energy** generates universal forces, including electricity and solar heat
5. **Force and matter are different forms of one and the same thing**: matter is stable, while heat, light, and electricity are unstable manifestations
6. **All elements are radioactive** in the sense that all dissociate spontaneously, only their rates differ
7. **The ether and matter are one thing under different forms**
8. **Equilibrium between matter and ether** can be locally lost (yielding new forms) or restored (returning to ether)

These map directly onto Larson's "motion is primary" thesis — except Le Bon reaches the conclusion empirically (from observations of radioactivity, X-rays, cathode rays) while Larson reaches it postulationally.

## Vortex theory of matter (Book III)

Book III, Chapter III, p. 94 (1907 ed.) is titled *Matter a particular state of ethereal Equilibrium*, and contains:

> "the elements ... are constituted by vortices formed in the midst of ether"
> (p. 11)

> "vortex ring theory — Explains Gravitation"
> (p. 94 chapter heading)

Le Bon endorses the **Helmholtz–Kelvin vortex-atom hypothesis**: atoms are stable vortex structures in a frictionless ether. This is the lineage that flows into:

- Larmor (1894+) — electron as rotational strain in elastic ether
- Larson (1959+) — atoms as compound rotations in the space-time motion field
- Peret (RS2, 2000s) — same atoms, but with the rotation explicitly quaternion-valued

## Dematerialization (Book IV)

The most-quoted Le Bon claim. Six chapters detail the radioactive transition matter → "imponderable" emanation → ether. Key formulation:

> "matter, ordinarily and naturally, spontaneously dissociated into etheric fragments"

> "all materials are radioactive"

The reviewer in Borderland Sciences (Vol. 46 No. 2) summarizes: "Matter and ether [are] interchangeable by nature."

## What we did NOT find

- No verbatim `p/v = M` equation in either book.
- No explicit derivation chain from p/v = M to a numerical mass value.
- No mention of the strong force, gluons, or anything resembling the modern QCD picture (Le Bon predates this by half a century).
- No predictions in MeV — Le Bon uses ergs and CGS throughout.
