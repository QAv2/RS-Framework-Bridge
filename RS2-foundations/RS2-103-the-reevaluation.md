# RS2-103: The Reevaluation of the Reciprocal System

## Core thesis

103 announces RS2's two changes to Larson's 1979 postulates: (a) drop "physical" so the postulates apply to all levels of existence; (b) replace Euclidean with projective geometry; (c) drop "commutative" because polar/rotational motion is non-commutative. Projective geometry stratifies the levels of existence (inanimate = Euclidean, biologic = affine, scalar motion = projective stratum) and provides the long-missing mechanism to convert scalar motion into coordinate motion that Larson never supplied (he used probability).

## Key constructions

- **Projective geometry as primary stratum.** Larson's scalar motion is now placed *prior to* metric assumptions, in the projective stratum. Euclidean and affine strata are derived sub-cases. Replaces Larson's Euclidean commitment.
- **Geometric strata correspond to Levels of Existence.** Inanimate (Euclidean), Biologic (affine), Ethical (further projective stratification). Peret states projective stratification yields "seven possible levels," of which *Beyond Space and Time* documents three.
- **Complex / quaternion representation of motion.** Linear motion (point + direction) and rotational motion (roll/pitch/yaw) are recognized as geometric reciprocals — polar is the inverse of linear. RS2 explicitly adopts complex quantities and quaternions as primary computational tools (a major departure from Larson's slide-rule-era formalism).
- **Life unit.** A new motion type, motion *between* space and time (rather than in space or in time), defining the biologic realm. Larson named it; RS2 places it geometrically in the affine stratum.
- **Scalar-to-coordinate conversion via projection.** Projective geometry provides the explicit mechanism by which a scalar magnitude becomes a coordinate object — Larson previously had no mechanism beyond probability.
- **Drop of "commutative."** Because rotational/polar motion is inherently non-commutative (quaternions don't commute), the 1965 commutative clause is removed in RS2.
- **Drop of "physical."** Postulates now read "the universe" rather than "the physical universe," so they apply across all levels of existence without needing the 1995 metaphysical postulates as an add-on.

## Equations / formal relations

No equations in 103. The structural assertions:

- Polar geometry = geometric inverse of linear geometry.
- Projective stratum ⊃ affine stratum ⊃ Euclidean stratum (each is a sub-case of the prior).
- Larson's "scalar motion" lives at the projective stratum, before metric assumptions are imposed.

## Verbatim quotes (postulates and definitions)

The new RS2 Fundamental Postulates:

> I. The universe is composed of one component, motion, existing in three dimensions, in discrete units, and with two reciprocal aspects, space and time.
>
> II. The universe conforms to the relations of ordinary mathematics, its primary magnitudes are absolute, and its geometry is Projective.

Statement of the three changes:

> No more "physical"—the Postulates now function for the non-physical, or "metaphysical" universe. Euclidean geometry was changed to projective geometry, to allow for the stratification into the Levels of Existence, and "commutative" was dropped as it is inapplicable to motion that occurs in more than one dimension, such as the polar geometry of the life or ethical unit.

On the affine geometry of biologic realm (citing Nick Thomas):

> Studies in the biological realm by Nick Thomas show that the geometric relations are not Euclidean in nature, as you find in the inanimate realm, but affine—they appear as a scalar recursion. Some of the geometric assumptions made by Euclid do not hold true in living forms. This is because the type of motion is different.

On life unit:

> When it comes to the animate, living realm, a new kind of motion is introduced, a motion that is between space and time, which Larson defines as a "life unit."

On stratification:

> Larson's "Level I—Inanimate" realm of Larson's Postulates is in the Euclidean geometric stratum, the "Level II—Biologic" is in the affine geometric stratum, and Larson's scalar motion (or space-time) sits cleanly in the projective stratum, prior to geometric assumptions.

On scalar-to-coordinate gap:

> Larson never provided a mechanism to convert scalar motion into coordinate motion, other than probability. Projective geometry provides this solution, in enormous detail.

On polar/linear reciprocity:

> The computer imaging process discovered that they are actually geometric reciprocals of each other; polar geometry is the inverse of linear geometry, and what better tool than a "geometric reciprocal" for a Reciprocal System of theory?

Larson on the need for reexamination (epigraph):

> "From time to time, therefore, it is well that we should undertake a critical reexamination of our fundamental theory in order to determine whether it is still adequate to carry the additional burdens that our more advanced facilities for observation and measurement have placed upon it. Perhaps the branches of the tree may have become too numerous and heavy for the trunk to support."
