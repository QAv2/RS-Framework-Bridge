# RS2-109: Dimensional Thinking

## Core thesis

109 systematizes the dimensional structure of RS2 by mapping Larson's units of motion to the four normed division algebras (1D real, 2D complex, 4D quaternion, 8D octonion). The progression of the natural reference system is the real-numbers unit; charged electrons/positrons are complex; particles/atoms are quaternionic (Larson's "double rotating system"); life units are octonionic. Variable units-per-dimension replaces Larson's fixed three-units-per-dimension model and homogenizes the three scalar dimensions.

## Key constructions

- **Division-algebra correspondence (Hurwitz / Frobenius theorem implicit).** Only 1, 2, 4, 8 dimensions support a normed division algebra. RS2 maps:
  - **1D (reals)** : progression of the natural reference system (outward unit speed, c). Never previously listed as a "unit of motion" by Larson.
  - **2D (complex)** : single rotating system — electrons / positrons.
  - **4D (quaternion)** : Larson's double rotating system — particles, atoms, photon, neutrinos.
  - **8D (octonion)** : interlinked double rotating systems — screw / vortex / helix structures, the "life unit" / α-helix.
- **Dual forms (paired algebras) for the rotational structures.**
  - Dual complex → birotation = vibrational motion of charge.
  - Dual quaternions → atomic structure (two double rotating systems, starting with proton).
  - Dual octonions → biological cell (couples material and cosmic structures).
  - Pairing connector: epsilon function ε (Grassmann/dual numbers).
- **Quaternion units mapped to Larson's four units of motion in one scalar dimension.**
  - 1 (real, +1) : outward speed (progression).
  - i (1D imaginary) : 1-x electric rotation (1D energy).
  - i·j (2D imaginary) : 2-x magnetic rotation (2D energy).
  - i·j·k = -1 : 3-x inward speed (gravity).
  Net effect: all three scalar dimensions become homogeneous (no special split of the 2nd dimension).
- **3-x speed range explained.** Inward unit speed (gravity) emerges naturally as i·j·k = -1 in the quaternion structure, replacing Larson's ad-hoc "incrementing the 2nd unit into the first unit of a 2nd dimension and reversing scalar direction."
- **Quaternion photon = electromagnetic.** With i·j = k, pairing i·j with -k rotation gives (k)(-k) = +1 — two counter-rotating systems (Nehru's birotation) producing a cosine wave at unit speed. The 2D magnetic rotation coupled to 1D electric rotation makes the photon EM radiation by structure. This naturally yields TE / TM / TEM transverse modes (absent from Larson's and Nehru's models).
- **Equivalent space as the imaginary aspect.** Energy n/1 expressed in equivalent space is a 2nd-power function (orbital v²), so equivalent space = Larson's "imaginary number" axis.
- **Rotation as primary motion (Nehru).** Verbatim: "Rotation is precluded only if space is one-dimensional. Just as soon as it is established that the stationary spatial reference system is three-dimensional, rotation becomes a possibility." Replaces Larson's direction-reversal-then-rotation construction.
- **1D-thinking critique.** Larson's framework was 1D-linear; Nehru extended to 2D complex; RS2 extends to 3D (quaternion) and beyond.

## Verbatim quotes (definitions)

Nehru on rotation as primary:

> "Rotation is precluded only if space is one-dimensional. Just as soon as it is established that the stationary spatial reference system is three-dimensional, rotation becomes a possibility"

On the imaginary aspect:

> Energy, having its "direction reversal" in time (n/1, the 1 being +1 -1 +1 = 1) had to be expressed in equivalent space, which is a 2-dimensional form of space representing the effect time has upon space. Speed, in space, was a linear translation (v), but because equivalent space was a 2nd power function, speed in equivalent space is orbital (v²), making equivalent space Larson's version of the "imaginary" number.

On division algebras:

> Continued investigation found that there were only four dimensional structures that were solvable, 1, 2, 4 and 8 dimensions, known today as division algebra.

The four-unit quaternion mapping:

> • The first unit is outward speed—a real magnitude that is the progression of the natural reference system, underlying all motion.
>
> • The second unit (1-x) is 1-dimensional (1.i) and imaginary, expressing energy as an electric rotation.
>
> • The third unit (2-x) is 2-dimensional (1.i.j) and imaginary, expressing energy as a magnetic rotation.
>
> • The last unit (3-x) is back to a 1-dimensional, real magnitude of opposite direction to the first unit, an inward speed (1.i.j.k = -1).

Quaternion photon:

> In quaternion math, 2D rotation of i.j is equivalent to 1D rotation of k; i.j = k. If i.j is paired with a -k rotation, you end up with (k)(-k) = +1, two counter-rotating systems (Nehru's birotating photon) producing a cosine wave that has a net, outward, unit speed in space: a wave traveling at the speed of light.

The hierarchy summary:

> The 1-dimensional, real numbers represent the outward expansion of the universe at unit speed, the speed of light. This "unit of motion," though used in the Reciprocal System frequently, was never defined as a unit of motion. The 2-dimensional, complex numbers represent a single, electric rotating system, electrons and positrons. The 4-dimensional quaternion represents Larson's "double rotating system," that forms the basis of particles and atoms, and electromagnetic in nature. Quaternions model the photon and neutrinos. The 8-dimensional octonian represents an interlinked structure of double rotating systems that are the mathematical model of a screw, vortex or helix. These structures are basic to life units, such as the α-helix of the protein.

Dual structures:

> Dual complex forms the birotation necessary for the vibrational motion of charge. Dual quaternions are the "two double-rotating systems" that form atomic structure, starting with the proton. Dual octonians express the life unit, the biological cell, linking the material and cosmic structures together, combining the linear motion of space with the rotational motion of time.

## Riemann-relevance flags

- **i·j·k = -1 chain.** The 4-unit progression {+1, i, i·j, -1} traverses the unit circle through quarter-turns. The middle unit i·j (= k) is the 2D magnetic rotation; the involution that takes +1 → -1 happens through *two* imaginary steps. This is structurally close to a "halfway" reciprocal-involution argument.
- **No explicit ½.** As in 105, 107, 108: the number 1/2 does not appear as a constant. The closest structural analogue is "spin-½" (107) and the i·j midpoint here.
- **Reciprocal involution s/t ↔ t/s.** Recapitulated; central to the speed/energy duality.
- **Octonion mention.** Notable because the only non-associative normed division algebra; relevant if the derivation needs to consider why 3D scalar motion is special (the quaternion = 4D = 3 imaginary units = 3D rotation group).
